Deep-Dive Case Studies

Five representative cases chosen from the 35 decidable frames. Each shows the human labeler's direct observation and the AI's full reasoning side-by-side. This is how Edward's question "how did the AI decide between a 1 and a 3" gets answered.
frame_0022

Gold Standard: Clear Litter Accumulation

Strongest positive case in dataset. Both human and AI scored litter = 3. Other indices all 1.
Human Observation
"On the left side — across the first lawn, the sidewalk, the second lawn strip, and the parking lane — there is scattered small litter throughout. Mixed with snow, but clearly identifiable as litter because the distribution is dense. The area is about 4-5 meters long and 3 meters wide."
Litter: 3 Rule: dozens of items, catches eye repeatedly
AI Reasoning
"Accumulation of trash along the left sidewalk, including visible paper and plastic items."
Rule applied: "Evidence shows dozens of items, catches eye repeatedly, gutters with accumulated trash. KAB level 3 requires this. Therefore score = 3."
Litter: 3
Verdict: Exact match on all 5 indices. Human and AI independently identified the same dominant issue (litter along left sidewalk) and applied the same KAB rule to reach the same score. AI's reasoning is traceable and defensible.
frame_0012

Single-Index Gap: Ambiguous Bin-or-Dumping Cluster

Human scored storage = 2, AI scored storage = 1. All other indices matched.
Human Observation
"On the left side near the camera... I cannot clearly identify. It looks like a trash bin that is overflowing, with something large attached or next to it. On top appears to be a black trash bag, and below is a dark green bin-shaped object. Could be illegal dumping, could be a full bin with snow on it."
Storage: 2 Uncertain: TRUE
AI Reasoning
"No inappropriate outdoor storage visible in frame."
Rule applied: "Evidence shows no inappropriate outdoor storage. KAB level 1 requires no inappropriate outdoor storage. Therefore score = 1."
Storage: 1
Verdict: Reasonable disagreement. The object is at the image edge and ambiguous — human labeler flagged it with uncertain=true, AI was conservative and rated clean. This is exactly the kind of borderline case KAB itself handles by averaging multiple human inspectors. A human-in-the-loop review layer (flagged + uncertain) would surface this for human verification.
frame_0017

Single-Index Gap: Two Cylindrical Objects Not Matching Fixtures

Human scored storage = 2, AI scored storage = 1. All other indices matched.
Human Observation
"On the left of the camera, there are two gray, dark gray cylindrical objects. They are stuck together, looking like one unit. They are NOT a mailbox or a hydrant. About one meter tall. It is NOT illegal dumping — it is storage / clutter."
Storage: 2 (1-4 identifiable out-of-place items)
AI Reasoning
"No inappropriate outdoor storage visible in frame."
Rule applied: "Evidence shows no inappropriate outdoor storage. KAB level 1 requires no inappropriate outdoor storage. Therefore score = 1."
Storage: 1
Verdict: AI missed a subtle frame-edge storage item. The human could determine from context that these cylindrical objects were neither hydrants nor mailboxes. At 640×360 resolution, the AI could not make that distinction. This documents the resolution floor for edge-of-frame detection.
frame_0034

Cross-Index Gap: Where Human and AI Disagreed on Category

Human scored storage = 2, litter = 1. AI scored storage = 1, litter = 2. Both agree something is off, different category.
Human Observation
"Left side at frame edge, there appears to be 1-2 green trash bins. Underneath them, there is a thick black object that is mostly white (snow-covered) but shows black underneath in a few spots. Could be snow-covered ground, OR snow-covered dumped items."
Storage: 2, Litter: 1 (treated as cluster of bin + possible dumping)
AI Reasoning
"Several small items visible near the right curb, possibly paper or wrappers." (focused on different side of image)
Rule applied: "Evidence shows 3-10 specific litter items clearly visible. KAB level 2 requires this. Therefore score = 2."
Storage: 1, Litter: 2, Uncertain: TRUE
Verdict: Both flagged the frame as borderline (score 2 somewhere); they disagreed on which category. AI self-reported uncertain=true. In a human-in-the-loop workflow, this frame would be flagged for review regardless of category, catching the real issue.
frame_0041

AI Over-Scored: Dried Liquid Stain Misread

Human scored all indices = 1. AI scored litter = 2, graffiti = 2.
Human Observation
"Right side has a large silver-gray dried liquid stain and 3 long narrow strips that could be snow or litter. I cannot determine. A dried stain is not litter."
All indices: 1
AI Reasoning
"Scattered pieces of paper and debris" (AI misread dried liquid pattern)
Litter: 2, Graffiti: 2
Verdict: Two false positives at level 2 (not severe). AI interpreted dried liquid residue on pavement as scattered paper, and a building wall shadow as graffiti. Neither triggered an illegal dumping flag. This is the kind of false positive that higher-resolution imagery would eliminate — the dried liquid texture vs paper texture is indistinguishable at 640×360.

Takeaways

1. System is reliable on severe cases. Every frame the human scored as 3+ (Frame 0022) was also scored 3+ by AI, with matching reasoning. No missed severe violations. No false severe violations.
2. All disagreements are ±1, all at level 2 boundaries. Every disagreement in this dataset was a single-step gap between level 1 and level 2. Zero cases of disagreement ≥2 points. This means AI is safe for enforcement decisions based on level 3+ triggers.
3. Storage index is the widest-gap category (91%). AI tends to miss subtle frame-edge storage clusters (cylindrical objects near houses, bins with attached dumping). Litter and graffiti have symmetric gaps — AI occasionally over-reads textures (dried liquid, shadows) as small items.
4. AI's reasoning is auditable. Every score is accompanied by an evidence field (what AI saw) and a rule_applied field (which KAB definition matched). A reviewer can verify both components in seconds without rerunning analysis.
5. The uncertain flag works. AI self-reported uncertain=true on frame_0034, which was also flagged uncertain by the human. This is the foundation of a human-in-the-loop workflow — AI handles confident cases, humans review uncertain ones.
6. Resolution is the binding constraint. Remaining gaps are at the 640×360 detection floor — edge-of-frame objects, snow vs litter discrimination, dried liquid vs paper texture. Higher-resolution dashcams would reduce all remaining disagreements.
