Key Findings

89% overall accuracy across 36 decidable frames (excluding 3 frames where human reviewer also could not determine if spots were snow or trash).

Zero false positives at HIGH level. The system never sent a severe alert for a location that was actually clean. All 3 HIGH detections confirmed as genuine problems by human review.

Two false positives at MEDIUM level: Frame 0024 (trash bin misidentified as trash bags) and Frame 0041 (dried liquid residue interpreted as litter).

Two-step improvement: Frame 0017 had a gray pile on the sidewalk that single-step missed entirely. Two-step detected it (L:2). Two-step also correctly reclassified 6 clean frames that single-step had flagged as LOW.

One missed detection in both systems: Frame 0012 has a small green pile at the left edge of frame. Difficult to see at 640×360. Noted as a resolution limitation.

Frames 0047-0048 are a garbage collection facility, not a residential street. Both systems correctly detected clutter, but this location should be excluded from residential analysis. City should provide facility location list for filtering.

Resolution Constraint

At 640×360, a plastic bottle occupies approximately 3-5 pixels. In 3 of 39 frames, the human reviewer could not distinguish snow patches from small litter items on a full-screen monitor. This is a physics limitation, not a software limitation. No AI model can extract information that does not exist in the image. Higher-resolution cameras (960×540 or above) would immediately improve detection accuracy for small items.

The system is most reliable for: clearly clean streets (L:1), heavy litter (L:3-4), large item dumping, and outside storage. The boundary between L:1 and L:2 is inherently ambiguous at this resolution for both AI and human observers.
